Hello, and thanks for stopping by.

My name is Lexi, some people know me as Lexii or 0x800a6. I’ve been building a platform called VTubers.TV for quite a while. I want to be honest with you about what this is, why it matters, and where it’s going.

## What is VTubers.TV?

VTubers.TV is a social platform for VTubers and the people who support them. It mixes the good parts of Twitch, YouTube, and X, but it's made for creators who have been pushed aside or ignored by those same places.

The idea came from a friend of mine. Let’s call her Jane. She streamed on YouTube using a nun-style VTuber model, fully covered, very modest. One day she was banned for "nudity and sexual content." No warning. No conversation. Just gone. Anyone who saw her content knew the ban didn’t make sense.

Watching that happen to her felt wrong. I kept thinking, “How many other small creators just disappear like this, with no explanation?” That’s when I decided to do something. Not just complain. Build.

## Why does this platform matter?

Platforms today feel cold and corporate. They’re run by algorithms that punish creators without understanding them. On VTubers.TV, that doesn’t happen. Here's what we do differently:

- **We never ban without reason.** We use AI and machine learning to help moderators, but they never make decisions. Every action is reviewed by a human.
  
- **We support NSFW content with clear rules.** If a stream or post is 18+, it must be marked correctly. The viewer and creator both have to verify their age. This keeps things safe, honest, and respectful.

- **We don’t tolerate hate.** No doxxing. No harassment. No homophobia, transphobia, or similar garbage. Everyone deserves to feel safe here.

- **We draw a clear line.** We do not allow lolicon or shotacon in NSFW contexts. Period.

This isn’t just a reaction to one bad event. It’s a response to the pattern: VTubers being misunderstood, misrepresented, or mistreated by systems that don’t care to understand them.

## What does it run on?

Behind the scenes, VTubers.TV is built with:

- Rust  
- Go  
- TypeScript  
- Next.js  
- TailwindCSS  
- MongoDB  
- C and C++  
- WebAssembly  

It’s fully open source, and licensed under the [MIT License](https://opensource.org/license/mit?ref=0x800a6.dev). That means anyone can inspect the code, help improve it, or run their own version if they choose.

## The funding problem

Now for the hard part. This isn’t free to run.

Storing video, running real-time chat, keeping the servers stable, these things aren’t cheap. A proper setup will easily cost thousands of dollars a month once users start joining. Right now, I’m covering the cost out of my own pocket. No big sponsors. No investors asking me to harvest data or throw in shady ad tech.

That gives me freedom to build the platform the right way. But it also means I can’t do it forever alone. If the project grows, I’ll need support. I’d rather ask for that up front than pretend the money part doesn’t exist.

## Release Date

Sometime before the end of the year.

## Who is this for?

- VTubers who are tired of worrying about unfair bans.
- Fans who just want to follow their favorite streamers without interruptions.
- Creators who want control over how they share content, including NSFW.
- Anyone who wants to be part of a platform that puts people first, not algorithms.

This isn’t just a project. It’s a place where people can be themselves, where rules are clear, and where you don’t vanish without warning because a bot made the wrong call.

Thanks for reading. If this sounds like something you care about, I hope you’ll stick around.
